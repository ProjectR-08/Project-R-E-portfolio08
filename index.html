What is E-Portfolio

It is an electronic porfolio (e-portfolio) is a purposeful collection of sample student work, demonstrations, and artifacts that showcase student's learning progression, achievement, and evidence of what students can do. The collection can include essays and papers (text-based), blog, multimedia (recordings of demonstrations, interviews, presentations, etc.), graphic (Berkeley 2021).

It can help learners to develop their skills and talent. That can be shared to friends, families, and showcase learner's in achievement when they are applying for a job - in short, it can be a simple or more advanced folder where all of your achievements, informations, about yourself, etc are included.

On the other hand, e-portfolio is a software based application or web based designed to replicate a learner's portfolio into a form of online processing. In which it allows public (without exception) to remotely accessed into it but in a secure server also, it is to prevent the copyright and stealing your private info that can be used by hackers or any malicious people around you to exposed you to through bad self.

Some of the advantages of e-portfolio includes;

  1. A reduction in costs of assessment and quality assurance
  2. The ability for learners to upload information whenever they are at a computer
  3. The facility for internal and external quality assurance
  4. The facility for the learners' Line Manager to check progress
  5. The ability to store electronic evidence such as video, sound files or photographs
  6. The reduced impact of ‘lost portfolios’
  7. The safe storage of current and archived portfolios.

"House at Night"

Artwork 2: Expressionism

“Woman with Black Glove”
by Albert Gleizes

Artwork 2: Cubism

In French, Woman with Black Glove is Femme au gant noir or Femme Assise. It is a painting by French artist, theorist, and writer Albert Gleizes. It consists of experimental style that transpired during the second synthetic phase of Cubism, called Crystal Cubism. I chose this artwork because of its dialogue emerging between the limits of the medium of painting and the fullness of space.

How did I make this Reponsive E-Portfolio?


At first, maybe some of you don't know how I made this e-portfolio.

In here, I am gonna share my experience in doing its.

First, I looked some free open source responsive template repository in google.

Second, after I found what tempate I'd liked to used - I downloaded it.

Third, after I've downloaded the template. I modified it - but of course with the help of my knowledge in HTML and CSS.

Fourth, I created a repository in my Github account. But for reason, I already have an account, so, in my case - I only added some commitment into my repository. At first, while I am committing into my repository, I experienced some problems. My website source was exceeded to 1GB plus, but the github storage was limited to 25MB. But for reason, I figured it out to prevent this. I decided that all of my videos that was embedded to my website was to be uploaded to my youtube account. So that, storage that github wanted will be accepted.

Fifth. I pulled and pushed my modified website to my repository in github that I have created.

Sixth, afted I pushed and pulled it in my reposity, I also modified my link to make it liked a unique website.

Bwalaaaaa! Now you have your source code uploaded to the google. With this, everyone can search your site.

The only reason why I made it on a hard way than using an auto-webstite-builder like wix, wordpress, or any free websites that supports in creating website is that, I wanted to understand how the responsives work and make, and of course I am looking for an experience to support my future website. Coz, maybe later - maybe next year - I will build some website where every students can used to it and even teachers or instructor around the internet, but that is for next season.
